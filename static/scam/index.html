My Story: The 2017 Cryptocurrency Scam

In late 2017, I found myself caught up in a perfect storm—an emotional rollercoaster in the wild, unregulated world of cryptocurrencies. Like so many others, I was chasing the dream, riding the wave of what felt like an unstoppable revolution. With Bitcoin rising rapidly, I decided to invest in what seemed like a golden opportunity: a bulk order of graphics cards. The goal was simple: sell high-demand GPUs and turn a massive profit.

But that dream quickly became a nightmare. I sent Bitcoin, hundreds of dollars’ worth, believing I was on the verge of striking it big. The supposed distributor kept stringing me along—there were delays at Mexican customs, then Texas customs—one excuse after another. Every request for more money came with a promise that the cards were almost in my hands. I wanted to believe so badly that I ignored the red flags.

The Bitcoin was gone, and I was left with nothing. No cards, no profit—just the sinking realization that I'd been scammed. Looking back, the signs were there all along. But the pull of success, the fear of missing out on this once-in-a-lifetime chance, clouded my judgment. This experience didn't just cost me financially—it hit me deeply, teaching hard lessons about trust and the dangers of unchecked ambition.
